Background

Pertussis (known as whooping cough) is a highly contagious respiratory disease caused by the bacterium Bordetella pertussis. People of all ages can be infected leading to violent coughing fits followed by a high-pitched “whoop” like intake of breath. Children have the highest risk for severe complications and death. Recent estimates from the WHO indicate that ~16 million cases and 200,000 infant deaths are due to pertussis annually.

Investigating pertussis cases by year

The United States Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) has been compiling reported pertussis case numbers since 1922 in their National Notifiable Diseases Surveillance System (NNDSS). We can view this data on the CDC website here: https://www.cdc.gov/pertussis/surv-reporting/cases-by-year.html

We can “scrape” the data with the datapasta package.

A tale of two vaccines (wP & aP)

Two types of pertussis vaccines have been developed: whole-cell pertussis (wP) and acellular pertussis (aP). The first vaccines were composed of ‘whole cell’ (wP) inactivated bacteria. The latter aP vaccines use purified antigens of the bacteria.

Q3. Describe what happened after the introduction of the aP vaccine? Do you have a possible explanation for the observed trend?

After the aP vaccine was introduced in 1996, pertussis cases started increasing again. This may be because the acellular vaccine provides shorter-lasting immunity than the earlier whole-cell vaccine, allowing immunity to wane over time.

Q14. What antigens show differences in the level of IgG antibody titers recognizing them over time? Why these and not others?

IgG titers change the most over time for antigens directly related to Bordetella pertussis and the vaccine, such as PT and FIM2/3. These show differences because the immune system was specifically exposed to them through vaccination or boosting, whereas control or unrelated antigens are not expected to trigger the same response.

The control antigen OVA does not show much change over time, but PT shows a clear rise in antibody levels after boosting. This suggests that PT is strongly recognized by the immune system, while OVA is not expected to change because it is not part of the vaccine.

Not exactly. The RNA expression pattern and antibody titer trend are related, but they do not peak at the same time because gene expression happens before the final antibody proteins build up in circulation.
